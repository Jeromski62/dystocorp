# Mega Corps

The power of the corporates extends across various sectors. In addition to the already mentioned areas of tech production, data mining, the arms trade and the hiring of mercenaries, there are other dominant sectors:

- **The energy and raw materials sector:**
A company controls energy production and the extraction of valuable raw materials. It may control solar energy, nuclear energy or alternative energy sources.

- **The telecommunications and information sector:** 
A company has control over communication networks, data centres and access to information. It could be a monopolistic internet or communications company.
- **The transport and logistics sector:** 
A company controls the space or interstellar transport and logistics of goods and people. It may control fleets of spaceships, hyperloop systems or other innovative means of transport.
- **The entertainment and media industry:** 
A company controls the production and distribution of entertainment media such as films, music, games and virtual reality. It is a powerful media conglomerate.
- **The financial and banking sector:** 
A company has control over the economy and the banking system. It could be a monopolistic bank or a global finance company.

These sectors are subject to constant power struggles and conflicts. Industrial espionage, economic warfare and even open armed conflicts are part of everyday life. Corporates fight for supremacy in their respective sectors and try to consolidate their power and monopoly.

In this universe of corporate dominance and relentless competition, people are fighting for their survival and their position in a society characterised by the imperatives of corporations and the challenges of space-time. The consequences for society are profound, and people must learn to navigate this new reality while fighting for their autonomy and personal goals.

There are major differences in the level of knowledge and distribution of information within these corporations. The spread across the star systems makes communication difficult and creates enormous time delays. The time dilation involved in travelling at almost the speed of light means that travellers have to be put into a kind of cryosleep to bridge the multi-year journeys. But when they arrive, they realise that the people they knew are already very old or even deceased. Planned arrivals lose their meaning as time passes relative to the destinations on the journeys
