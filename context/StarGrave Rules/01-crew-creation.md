# Crew Creation

> Quelle: `stargrave_crew_creation.pdf` (Buchseiten 11–28, Kapitel 1: "Assembling a Crew")

A crew consists of exactly **1 Captain**, exactly **1 First Mate**, and up to **8 Soldiers**
(of which max. 4 may be Specialists) — 10 permanent members total. Additional
**Temporary Crewmembers** can join for a single game via powers, tech, or scenario rules.

## Creation order

1. Pick a mental concept for the crew (no rules impact, flavor only).
2. Create the **Captain**: species/name (flavor only) → Background → Powers (5) → Stats →
   Level 15 → Gear (6 slots).
3. Create the **First Mate**: Background (independent choice) → Powers (4) → Stats →
   Level 0 → Gear (5 slots).
4. Recruit up to 8 **Soldiers** with a starting budget of **400 credits (cr)**.

---

## Stat-line

Every figure (captain, soldier, creature) has 6 stats:

| Stat | Abbr. | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| Move | M | Distance a figure can move per action |
| Fight | F | Melee skill and defense vs. incoming fire |
| Shoot | S | Skill with ranged weapons |
| Armour | A | Physical protection (armour + shields) |
| Will | W | Determination, courage, tech aptitude |
| Health | H | Damage a figure can take before badly wounded/killed |

Higher is always better. Fight/Shoot/Will are usually written as modifiers (e.g. `+3`);
Move/Armour/Health are absolute numbers.

### Split Stats

Sometimes a stat has two values, written `actual/effective` (e.g. `+2/+3`). The first
number is the figure's real stat; the second is its current effective value under some
temporary condition (advanced tech, an active power, an injury).

## Captain

### Base stat-line (before background modifiers)

| M | F | S | A | W | H |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 6 | +3 | +2 | 9 | +3 | 16 |

- **Level**: starts at **Level 15** (already "baked in" — no separate leveling-up needed to
  reach this; see errata). See `03-rules-quick-reference.md` / campaign rules for further leveling.
- **Gear**: 6 gear slots, chosen freely from the General Equipment List, no cost beyond slot usage.
  Gear can be swapped freely between games in a campaign.
- **Powers**: choose **5** powers total.
  - **3 or 4** (player's choice) must come from the background's **Core Powers** list.
  - The remaining **1 or 2** must be chosen from any other power in the full power catalog
    (`02-powers-catalog.md`), excluding the background's core powers.
  - **Activation Number** for each chosen power:
    - Core power → activation number as printed.
    - Non-core power → printed activation number **+2**.
  - After assigning all 5, the player may reduce the activation number of **2 different
    powers by 1 each** (regardless of core/non-core status).
  - **Strain** value is fixed per power regardless of core/non-core status (never modified).

### Choosing a Background

Background determines stat modifications and the pool of Core Powers. All 8 are
open to both Captain and First Mate independently (they don't need to match).

| Background | Stat Modifications | Core Powers |
|---|---|---|
| **Biomorph** | +1 Health, and choose two of: +1 Move, +1 Fight, +1 Shoot | Adrenaline Surge, Armour Plates, Camouflage, Fling, Regenerate, Restructure Body, Toxic Claws, Toxic Secretion |
| **Cyborg** | +1 Health, and choose two of: +1 Move, +1 Fight, +1 Shoot | Camouflage, Control Robot, Data Knock, Energy Shield, Power Spike, Quick-Step, Target Lock, Temporary Upgrade |
| **Mystic** | +2 Will, +1 Health, and choose one of: +1 Move, +1 Fight, +1 Shoot | Control Animal, Dark Energy, Heal, Life Leach, Mystic Trance, Puppet Master, Suggestion, Void Blade |
| **Robotics Expert** | +1 Will, and choose two of: +1 Move, +1 Fight, +1 Shoot, +1 Health | Control Robot, Create Robot, Drone, Electromagnetic Pulse, Remote Firing, Remote Guidance, Repair Robot, Re-wire Robot |
| **Rogue** | +1 Will, +1 Health, and choose two of: +1 Move, +1 Fight, +1 Shoot | Bait and Switch, Bribe, Cancel Power, Concealed Firearm, Data Jump, Fortune, Haggle, Quick-Step |
| **Psionicist** | +2 Will, +1 Health, and choose one of: +1 Move, +1 Fight, +1 Shoot | Break Lock, Destroy Weapon, Lift, Psionic Fire, Psychic Shield, Pull, Suggestion, Wall of Force |
| **Tekker** | +2 Will, and choose two of: +1 Move, +1 Fight, +1 Shoot, +1 Health | Antigravity Projection, Data Jump, Data Knock, Data Skip, Drone, Electromagnetic Pulse, Holographic Wall, Transport |
| **Veteran** | +1 Fight, +1 Health, and choose one of: +1 Move, +1 Fight (total +2 Fight), +1 Shoot | Armoury, Command, Coordinated Fire, Energy Shield, Fortune, Power Spike, Remote Firing, Target Designation |

Background flavor summaries (for name/portrait generation, not mechanically relevant):

- **Biomorph** — genetically/surgically modified survivor, often on the run from creators or the law. Loose or stretchy clothing, custom armour.
- **Cyborg** — man-machine fusion, ex-military specialist now mercenary/bounty hunter/investigator. Appearance ranges from fully human-looking to fully robotic.
- **Mystic** — doomsday-cultist or monastic-order adept with reality-bending "magic". Loose robes, rarely wears armour (interferes with powers).
- **Robotics Expert** — master roboticist with empathic understanding of machines, crew often robot-heavy. Utilitarian look, carries tools/spare parts.
- **Rogue** — smuggler/gambler/fixer type, no supernatural powers, relies on wit and luck. Normal clothes, concealed weapon.
- **Psionicist** — rare psychic, actively hunted/recruited by pirate fleets. Shaved head (hair itches when using powers), unique tattoos, no armour.
- **Tekker** — repairs/reverse-engineers pre-collapse high technology. Carries lots of tech gear and tool kits.
- **Veteran** — war survivor, combat specialist, best gear/armour available. Various motivations from lost causes to mercenary work.

## First Mate

The captain's second-in-command. Created the same way as the Captain, but weaker.

### Base stat-line (before background modifiers)

| M | F | S | A | W | H |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 6 | +2 | +2 | 9 | +2 | 14 |

- **Level**: starts at **Level 0**.
- **Gear**: 5 gear slots, same rules as Captain gear.
- **Powers**: choose **4** powers total.
  - **2 or 3** (player's choice) from the background's Core Powers.
  - Remainder from any non-core power.
  - **Activation Number**:
    - Core power → printed activation number **+2**.
    - Non-core power → printed activation number **+4**.
  - (No activation-number reduction step for First Mates — that's Captain-only.)
  - Strain is fixed as printed, same as Captain.

## Soldiers

Recruited with a shared crew budget of **400 credits (cr)**. Max 8 soldiers total, max 4
of which may be from the Specialist table. Soldier stats are **fixed** (never chosen or
modified except by external effects like tech or powers). All soldiers get **1 extra gear
slot** beyond their listed starting gear, usable only for loot found/bought during a
campaign (and if used for a weapon/armour upgrade, must match the same weapon/armour type
already listed).

### Soldier gear rules

- Soldiers cannot buy additional starting gear from the General Equipment List.
- Each soldier has exactly 1 extra gear slot, usable only for campaign loot/purchases;
  weapon/armour upgrades in that slot must match the category already listed for that soldier.
- If a soldier loses gear listed in their profile, it's replaced for free after the game.

### Robots

Any newly recruited soldier can be declared a **Robot** for free (no cost, no stat/gear
change) — just add the "Robot" tag to their notes. Robots are immune to Wounding and Toxic
rules, unaffected by gas/low oxygen. Many powers only affect robots (or explicitly cannot
affect them). Captains and First Mates cannot be robots (use the Cyborg background instead
for a similar concept).

---

## Temporary Crewmembers

A crew is capped at 10 permanent members (1 Captain + 1 First Mate + 8 Soldiers). Powers,
advanced tech, or scenario rules can add **temporary members** who follow all normal rules
but leave the crew again at the end of that single game.

---

## Aliens (flavor rule)

Species/appearance of any figure (captain, first mate, soldier) is purely cosmetic — no
mechanical effect, as long as the figure is roughly human-sized. Non-humanoid aliens
typically appear as "uncontrolled creatures" (see Bestiary, not included in this context
set) rather than as playable crew. Players may reflavor any soldier/captain/first-mate
stat-block as any alien species they like.
